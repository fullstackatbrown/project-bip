Overall Purpose
In an age during which technology is inextricably linked to education, students are “plugged in” nearly all day long. Unfortunately, this comes with intrinsic drawbacks, among which are threats to student privacy. Too often, the same devices that are invaluable to academic pursuits can also be leveraged to compromise sensitive information, often without the knowledge of students or their parents as a result of opaque policies. From the implementation of training surrounding digital privacy to the need for legislation that requires administrative transparency, there are myriad promising policy solutions to this pressing issue. The Student Privacy research team, added to BIP during Summer 2021, will operate with the goal of collaborating with state and local stakeholders to explore and promote innovative means of addressing this challenge. The team will be recruiting analysts beginning in Fall 2021!
Short Term Goals
Launched in the Summer of 2021, BIP’s Student Privacy team will be working to advance several issues related to students’ rights in Rhode Island. Our analysts will begin by tackling the issue of student interrogation without a parent present by conducting a research study and developing an advocacy strategy alongside the ACLU. Starting in the Fall of 2022, it will then take its advocacy experience to support legislation protecting student data in the context of school over Zoom.
